# Training Materials for HEOR

Welcome! I am a Master's student in Public Health Science majoring in Health Economics, with a strong focus on data-driven research. 

This repository is a collection of materials developed during our team training sessions to solve a very practical problem: hospital datasets usually share the exact same data structure, yet research teams waste a massive amount of time writing separate code to process them. 

The goal of these resources is to get our multidisciplinary team on the same page, standardize our workflows, and drastically cut down the time spent on data cleaning and analysis using R and Excel. 

---

## 💡 What’s Inside?
All projects use **simulated or public data** and focus on real-world Health Economics and Outcomes Research (HEOR) methods. 

### Topic Focus:
* **Data Processing & Cleaning:** Workflows to get messy hospital data ready for analysis.
* **Data Visualization:** Turning raw numbers into clear, professional charts.
* **Data Analysis:** Statistical approaches commonly used on healthcare datasets.
* **Decision Modelling:** Simple Cost-Effectiveness Analysis (CEA) and Budget Impact Analysis (BIA) models.

---

## 🗺️ How to Use These Materials

Each project folder is a self-contained training module. 

### Recommended Step-by-Step Workflow:
1. **The Slides:** Start with the presentation deck to understand the logic behind the task.
2. **The Exercise:** Open the folder, load the dataset, and run the code.
3. **Reflect:** Check your results against the short explanations and key outputs provided.

### Every folder includes:
* 📊 **Slides:** The training presentations.
* 🔢 **Data:** The practice datasets.
* 💻 **Code:** Documented R scripts or Excel files.
* 📝 **Notes:** A quick summary of the analytical approach and the final outputs.
